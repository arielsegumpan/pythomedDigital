“Herb of balance” Balanced sugar + balanced weight = balanced life

Herbal Natural
October 31, 2023
100 comments

Eating sweets makes us happy* and cutting off sugars can be quite frustrating for some. Glucose, referred to as blood sugar, is an important source of energy for our body’s cells, tissues, and organs. The body uses carbohydrates to produce glucose and spares protein from being used to make glucose needed by the body. But too much intake of sugar or its poor metabolism by the body due to a deficiency in insulin makes us prone to diabetes. If this remains uncontrolled, it can result to disease complications.*Eating sweet food activates the brain’s reward system releasing dopamine that signals an event is positive.

Lagerstroemia speciosa (L.) Pers., commonly known as Banaba is a large, semi-deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia and widespread throughout the Philippine islands. This traditional Filipino antidiabetic plant was brought to the attention of the world in the 1940s from the work of a Filipino scientist. Since then, numerous researchers have reported the impressive activity of Banaba, particularly due to its active constituent corosolic acid in lowering blood sugar. In the last two decades, several new antidiabetic compounds have also been discovered, including gallotannins, ellagitannins, and ellagic acid. In all, over 40 health benefitting phytochemicals can be found in banaba. Of these, corosolic acid and ellagic acid stand out because of their well-studied bioactivities and their relative abundance in the plant.

Tuklas Lunas' standardized Banaba food supplement is formulated to contain both ethanolic and water extracts of Banaba leaves to capture its full spectrum of bioactivity. Unlike other Banaba supplements in the market, which are either made from powdered leaves or are standardized only for corosolic acid (an ethanol-based extract), this product is also standardized for ellagic acid, a definitive indicator of the presence of the water-soluble phytochemicals of Banaba. Tuklas Lunas' full spectrum Banaba exhibits beneficial properties to glucose regulation and metabolism, including enhanced cellular uptake of glucose, improved insulin sensitivity, and inhibited intestinal hydrolysis of sucrose. Beyond its antidiabetic activity, Banaba is also known for its anti-inflammatory, anti-microbial, antiviral, anti-hyperuricemia, anti-fibrotic, antinociceptive, anti-obesity, and antioxidant activities.
